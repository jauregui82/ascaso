Cookie Policy

In accordance with current legislation, the aim of this policy is to inform users about how and why we use cookies on the www.ascaso.com website (hereinafter ‘ASCASO’), and to request the user’s consent to our use of them.

ASCASO, like the majority of websites, uses cookies to improve and optimise the user’s experience. You will find detailed information below about the nature of cookies, which types of cookies are used by ASCASO, how you can disable cookies in your browser, and how to specifically block the installation of cookies by third parties.

WHAT ARE COOKIES AND HOW ARE THEY USED?

Cookies are small files that the website or application you are using installs in your browser or device (smartphone, tablet or Internet-connected TV) while you browse the website or the application, storing information about your visit. In common with the majority of sites on the Internet, ASCASO uses cookies to perform the following tasks:

– Ensure the website is functioning correctly

– Store your preferences, such as the language or the font size you have selected

– Track your browsing experience

– Gather anonymous statistical information, such as the pages the user has visited or how long they were logged in

ASCASO uses cookies to operate, adapt and make the user’s browsing of the site as straightforward as possible.

Cookies establish a link between an anonymous user and their computer or device without supplying any references from which personal data can be inferred. You may reconfigure your browser settings at any time to modify or block the installation of cookies sent by ASCASO without suffering any restriction to your ability to access the website content. The quality of the operation of services may be affected, however.

WHY ARE COOKIES IMPORTANT?

– From a technical point of view, cookies allow websites to operate more swiftly by tailoring the experience to the user’s preferences, for example, by storing the language preference or detecting the access device

– Cookies set levels of protection and security that prevent or hinder cyberattacks against ASCASO or its users

– Cookies allow media managers to analyse the statistical data they collect in order to improve the quality and experience of services

– Cookies can help to optimise the advertising we display on the site and personalise that advertising according to the user’s browsing habits

WHICH TYPES OF COOKIES ARE USED BY ASCASO?

Session cookies expire when the user leaves the website or closes the browser. In other words, they are active only for the duration of the visit and are erased from your computer when the user leaves the site.

Persistent cookies are a type of cookie whereby the data remains in the terminal and can be accessed and processed for a period determined by the entity in charge of the cookie, which can be anything from a few minutes to several years.

We can also make a distinction between own and third-party cookies, according to the entity that manages the computer or domain from which the cookies are managed and the collected data is processed.

– Own cookies are cookies linked to your computer and managed exclusively by ACASO to achieve the best possible operation of this website. The information we gather is used to improve the quality of our service.

– If you interact with the content of ASCASO, you will also receive third-party cookies (when, for example, you click on social media buttons or view videos hosted on another website) issued by a domain other than ASCASO. Entries on social media and the cookies associated with these are subject to their respective cookie policies.
